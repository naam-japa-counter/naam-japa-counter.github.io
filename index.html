What is a Naam Japa Counter?

More Than a Counting Tool — A Path to Devotion

A naam japa counter helps you stay disciplined in doing "Japa" — repeating a mantra or divine name with focus and devotion. Traditionally, people use a Mala (prayer beads) with 108 beads for counting. But in modern life, carrying a Mala everywhere is not always easy.

That is why a digital japa counter is very useful. Our naam jaap counter gives you a similar feeling like using a real Mala, along with the comfort of modern technology. You can track your sessions even without internet, check your mala counter progress, and slowly build a strong daily habit.

Whether you chant radha naam jap, Ram naam, or any other mantra, this mantra jap counter makes sure you never lose count. It brings devotion and remembrance into your pocket.

How to Use

How to Use the Naam Japa Counter App

We believe spiritual technology should be simple. Our jaap counter is easy to use, so you can focus on chanting, not on the screen.

1

Choose Your Mantra

When you open the app, you can select from ready mantras or add your own. Whether it is radha naam jap or any Vedic mantra, the app supports all.

2

Enter Focus Mode

For better concentration, use Focus Mode. This screen removes extra buttons and shows a big tapping area with a timer. You can track both count and time of your chanting.

3

Start Chanting

With every chant, tap the screen once. The jaap counter will increase with each tap. You will feel a soft vibration, just like moving one bead of a Mala.

4

Continuous Mala Counting

After every 108 chants, the mala counter increases by one automatically, and the japa count starts again from zero. This makes long sessions smooth and easy.

5

Review and Save

After finishing, press save. Your session will be stored in the history section. You can see your total naam jaap counter details, time spent, and mantra used.

Sacred Science

From Ancient Beads to Digital Counters

Japa is an ancient spiritual practice from India. The word comes from Sanskrit, meaning to softly repeat or chant. For thousands of years, people used Japa Malas made from Tulsi, Neem, or Rudraksha beads. These were believed to carry spiritual energy.

Today, life has changed, but our spiritual needs are the same. A naam japa counter is a modern form of this old tradition. Just like saints kept their malas safe in cloth bags, our digital japa counter gives you a clean, ad-free space inside your phone.

When you use a mantra jap counter, you are not only counting numbers. You are building discipline and devotion. Whether you chant radha naam jap for divine love or for peace of mind, keeping count helps your mind stay focused.

What is the difference between a Jaap counter and a Mala counter?

A jaap counter counts each chant. A mala counter counts every 108 chants as one round. In our app, both work together automatically — so you never lose track.

Can I use this as a Radha Naam Jap counter?

Yes. You can set radha naam jap as your mantra and track it separately every day. You can also add any mantra of your choice.

Can I add my own mantra?

Yes. You can add any mantra in the mantra jap counter and track it separately from other mantras in your daily sadhana.

Does the app work without internet?

Yes. The naam jaap counter works 100% offline. No internet connection is required at any time — your sadhana is never interrupted.

Go to settings and open the reminders section. Set the time, and your radha naam counter will remind you gently every day at your chosen time.
